When I miss a deadline, you get paid

I'm Matthew Gatland and I used to make tiny games all the time. But then something went wrong. I got tired - or lazy - or caught up in the other parts of life. I don't know what happened. All I know is this: for the first time since I was a child, I went 12 months without releasing a game.


You can help! I don't need your money. I just need pressure. I need a reason to get the job done, some reason I can't just put it off forever.

So here's the deal: from now on, every month, I will release a game. But if I don't, I'll pay each of my anti-patrons $5 New Zealand dollars.

If I miss the deadline, I have to pay up.


Hopefully that's enough pressure to force me back into action. If it's not, you'll find out in your monthy payment email!


Technical Details

Sending actual money is actually quite difficult. My current plan is to pay you in Steam credit. When you sign up I'll collect your Steam username and add you as a friend. Steam will, in theory, let me send out as many Steam gift cards as I like, as long as they are to people I was already friends with.


If this doesn't work - for example if Steam bans me for suspicious payment behaviour - we'll figure out something else.
